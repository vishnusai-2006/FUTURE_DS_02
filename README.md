# Customer Retention & Churn Analysis

## Objective
Analyze customer data to identify churn patterns, key retention drivers, and customer lifetime trends for a subscription-based telecom business.

---

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Google Colab

---

## Dataset Overview
The dataset contains customer demographic details, service usage information, contract type, tenure, monthly charges, and churn status.

Key features include:
- Contract Type
- Tenure
- Monthly Charges
- Total Charges
- Internet Service
- Churn

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Overall Churn Rate
- Approximately 26.6% of customers have churned.
- This indicates moderate customer loss requiring retention strategies.

### 2. Contract Type Impact
- Month-to-month customers show the highest churn.
- 1-year and 2-year contracts significantly reduce churn.

### 3. Tenure Impact
- Customers with lower tenure churn more.
- Long-term customers are more stable.

### 4. Monthly Charges Impact
- Higher monthly charge customers show higher churn tendency.
- Pricing plays a major role in retention.

### 5. Internet Service Impact
- Fiber optic customers have the highest churn rate.
- Service quality or pricing review may be required.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Promote long-term contracts.
- Improve onboarding for new customers.
- Review high-priced plans.
- Improve fiber optic service experience.
- Implement targeted retention campaigns.

---

## Conclusion

Customer churn is strongly influenced by contract type, tenure, pricing, and service type. Strategic retention initiatives can significantly improve long-term revenue stability.
